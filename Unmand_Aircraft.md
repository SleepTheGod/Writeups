# 4) Regulatory Framework

### 4.1 State vs Civil Aircraft

Under international aviation law (**Chicago Convention, Article 3**):

* Military aircraft are classified as **“State Aircraft”**
* State aircraft are **excluded from civil aviation regulatory frameworks**

---

### 4.2 FAA Jurisdiction

Civil aircraft:

* Must maintain FAA registration (N-number)
* Must broadcast compliant ADS-B in regulated airspace

Military aircraft:

* Operate under **Department of Defense (DoD) authority**
* Use **independent airworthiness and tracking systems**
* Are **not required to appear in FAA public registries**

---

### 4.3 ADS-B / Transponder Compliance

Military aircraft are permitted to:

* Limit or suppress ADS-B identity
* Use non-public ICAO addresses
* Operate via Mode S without full broadcast data

This is typically done for:

* Operational Security (OPSEC)
* Mission confidentiality
* Training realism
